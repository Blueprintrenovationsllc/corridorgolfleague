How It WorksThree Formats. One Round. No Passengers.

4-man teams compete in a split matchup format. Teammates ride together in their own cart — their two opponents ride in the adjacent cart. Both cart groups play the same match simultaneously on opposite sides of the course and converge mid-round. Every player locked in for all 18 holes. The pressure shifts every six holes. Nobody checks out.

Team RosterYour Team. Your Name. Your Identity.

Every team carries 6 members — 4 active players and 2 reserves. Teams are formed by friends. Each team chooses their own name and identity. A team captain manages the lineup each week, deciding who plays and who sits. Reserves attend all tournaments as spectators when not in the lineup.

03
Eligibility & Fair PlayHandicap Rules

The Corridor uses a verified net scoring system designed to protect competitive integrity across the full field. All scoring is net at 50% of your GHIN index. Strokes are distributed across all 18 holes by course stroke index — wherever the hardest holes fall across the three formats is where your strokes land.

1
GHIN Verified Index Required — All members must maintain an active GHIN handicap index through the USGA. Self-reported handicaps are not accepted. GHIN costs approximately $30–40/year through your home club or the USGA directly.
2
Maximum Index: 15 — Plus Handicaps Welcome — The Corridor is open to players with a verified GHIN index of + to 15. This protects the competitive standard, the pace of play, and the quality of the field.
3
50% Handicap Allowance — All net scoring is calculated at 50% of your GHIN index across all 18 holes. Strokes are distributed by the course stroke index card. A 15 index plays as a 7 or 8. A 10 plays as a 5. A scratch plays as scratch. Plus handicaps play to their plus allowance.
4
Team Composition — Minimum Combined 20 — The playing 4 each week must have a minimum combined handicap of 20 at 50% allowance. This prevents teams from stacking all low handicap players and keeps competition balanced across the full field.
5
Sandbagging Clause — Any player whose net scores consistently outperform their handicap by 3 or more strokes over multiple tournaments is subject to a mandatory handicap review. The league reserves the right to adjust playing handicaps to protect competitive integrity.

Optional Buy-In · Pro Shop VouchersThe Skins Competition

The skins competition runs across the entire field — not just your matched opponent. Every team that buys in competes on every hole against all other teams. Winners receive pro shop vouchers paid out at the end of the round. Same 50% handicap allowance applies. The format shifts every six holes matching the tournament structure.
