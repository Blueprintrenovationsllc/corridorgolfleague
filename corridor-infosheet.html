How It WorksThe Format

4-man teams compete in a split matchup format. Teammates ride together in their own cart — their two opponents ride in the adjacent cart. Both cart groups play the same match simultaneously on opposite sides of the course and converge mid-round. Every player locked in for all 18 holes.

Holes 1–6
BEST BALL

Each player plays their own ball. Best net score per hole counts for the team against the full field.

Holes 7–12
ALT SHOT

One ball, two players. Teammates alternate every stroke. One net score per cart group per hole.

Holes 13–18
INDIVIDUAL

Every player, every ball. Individual net scores count personally toward standings and skins.

02
Whole Field · Optional Buy-InThe Skins Game

The skins game runs across the entire field — not just your matched opponent. Every team that buys in competes on every hole against all other teams in the tournament. Skins are settled after the round once all scorecards are posted. The format changes every six holes — and so does how a skin is won.

Holes 1–6
BEST BALL SKINS
Team vs. Whole Field

The team's best net score on each hole competes against all other teams. Lowest net best ball in the field wins the skin. Tie = carries over to the next hole.

Split 4 Ways
Holes 7–12
ALT SHOT SKINS
Cart Group vs. Whole Field

Each cart group's alternate shot net score competes against all other cart groups in the field. Lowest net alt shot score wins the skin. Tie = carries over.

Split 2 Ways
Holes 13–18
INDIVIDUAL SKINS
Player vs. Whole Field

Every player competes individually. Lowest net score on each hole across the entire field wins the skin outright. The real money. Tie = carries over.

Winner Takes All
Carryover Rule

Ties carry over within each phase. A new phase always starts fresh — no carryover between Best Ball, Alt Shot, and Individual.

Scoring

All skins played on net scores using verified GHIN indexes. Handicap strokes applied per hole based on stroke index.

Settlement

Skins paid out at the end of the round once all scorecards are posted. Separate from the main tournament payout.

Eligibility & Fair PlayHandicap Rules
1
GHIN Verified Index Required — All members must maintain an active GHIN handicap index through the USGA. Self-reported handicaps are not accepted. GHIN costs approximately $30–40/year through your home club or the USGA directly.
2
Maximum Index: 18 — The Corridor is open to players with a verified GHIN index of 0–18. This keeps competition serious while remaining accessible to dedicated recreational golfers.
3
Net Scoring Throughout — All tournament scoring, skins, and season standings are calculated on a net basis. Handicap strokes applied per hole based on stroke index.
4
Sandbagging Clause — Any player whose net scores consistently outperform their handicap by 3 or more strokes over multiple tournaments is subject to a mandatory handicap review. The league reserves the right to adjust playing handicaps.
5
Balanced Team Assignment — Teams are assigned to ensure a balanced handicap mix. Each team will carry a range across the 0–18 index spectrum. No team may stack all low or all high handicap players.
